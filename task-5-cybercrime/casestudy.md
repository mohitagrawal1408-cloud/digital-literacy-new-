# Case Study: UPI and Screen-Sharing Fraud

## The Crime
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) Fraud facilitated through malicious screen-sharing applications and deceptive payment requests.

## How it Happens
The crime typically begins with a phone call from a scammer posing as a bank representative, telecom customer support, or online marketplace buyer. They create a sense of urgency, telling the victim that their KYC is pending or their account will be blocked unless immediate action is taken. 

The scammer then instructs the victim to download a screen-sharing app, such as AnyDesk or TeamViewer. Once installed, the scammer can see everything happening on the victim's phone screen. Next, the scammer sends a UPI "collect request" to the victim. Believing they are merely authorizing a small verification fee or receiving a refund, the victim opens their UPI app and types in their secret UPI PIN. 

Because the scammer is monitoring the screen live, they capture the PIN as it is entered. The scammer then uses the authorized request and the captured PIN to immediately drain funds from the victim's bank account.

## Who is Targeted
College students trying to quickly resolve technical issues, elderly individuals who are unfamiliar with digital banking security, and frequent online shoppers are the primary targets of this scam.

## The Consequences
Victims suffer immediate and severe financial loss, which can drain a student's entire bank balance. Beyond the monetary impact, this crime causes significant mental distress and involves a lengthy, often unsuccessful process of filing bank disputes and police reports to recover the stolen funds.
