# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

1. **Never enter a UPI PIN to receive funds:** A UPI PIN is strictly used to authorize sending money or checking your balance. You will never need to enter a PIN to receive a payment, scholarship, or refund.
2. **Deny unsolicited screen-sharing requests:** Never download remote access applications (like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or QuickSupport) at the request of an unknown caller, even if they claim to be official bank or telecom support.
3. **Verify UPI payment requests thoroughly:** Always double-check the recipient's actual name and Virtual Payment Address (VPA) displayed on your screen before approving any transaction or mandate.
4. **Secure your academic and personal accounts:** Use strong, unique passwords for your university portals, student email, and social media. Always enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) where available.
5. **Avoid unsecured public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks:** Do not log into banking applications or make online fee payments while connected to open, public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, transit stations, or campus common areas.
6. **Be highly highly skeptical of artificial urgency:** Scammers often panic their victims with threats of immediate account blocking or pending legal action. Always pause, disconnect the call, and independently verify the claim using official contact numbers.
7. **Beware of phishing links and fake job offers:** Do not click on suspicious links sent via SMS, WhatsApp, or email that offer unexpected part-time jobs, urgent KYC updates, or free gifts. 
8. **Report incidents immediately:** If you experience or suspect any form of cyber fraud, report it instantly to the National Cyber Crime Portal at **cybercrime.gov.in** or call the national 24x7 Helpline at **1930**.
