# Social Media Etiquette for College Students

## Do's:

1. Do curate a professional image: Treat public profiles (like LinkedIn) as a digital resume; highlight your academic projects, skills, and achievements.

2. Do review your privacy settings regularly: Ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts, photos, and location data on platforms like Instagram or Facebook.

3. Do verify information before sharing: Check the credibility of news or articles before reposting to avoid spreading misinformation or fake news.

4. Do engage respectfully: When commenting or participating in online discussions, maintain a polite and constructive tone, even if you disagree.

5. Do assume everything is permanent: Remember that screenshots exist; think carefully before posting anything you wouldn't want a future employer or professor to see.

## Don'ts:

1. Don't overshare personal details: Avoid posting your exact location in real-time, your home address, or your phone number publicly.

2. Don't engage in cyberbullying or toxic arguments: Never use social media to harass, insult, or demean peers or faculty members.

3. Don't post inappropriate content: Refrain from sharing content that features illegal activities, hate speech, or highly unprofessional behavior.

4. Don't complain about your college or professors publicly: Venting frustrations online can quickly damage your reputation and potentially violate university codes of conduct.

5. Don't ignore platform guidelines: Always adhere to the terms of service of the platforms you use to avoid account suspension or bans.
